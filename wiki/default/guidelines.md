# Clinical Guidelines

## Cardiovascular
### Heart Failure (HF)
- [ID: 2f6961] 2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Guideline for the Management of Heart Failure
  - Key Recommendation: Quadruple therapy (ARNI, Beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i) for HFrEF.
  - Decision: Adopted
  - Physician Notes: I always check BMP at 1 week after starting ARNI/MRA.
  - Rationale: High risk of hyperkalemia and AKI in my patient population (predominantly CKD).
  - Added: 2026-05-27

### Atrial Fibrillation (AFib)
- [ID: c94385] 2023 ACC/AHA AFib Guideline
  - Key Recommendation: Early rhythm control for symptomatic AFib.
  - Decision: Deferred
  - Physician Notes: Defer to cardiology for rhythm control if patient is >80yo.
  - Rationale: Early rhythm control is preferred, but rate control is acceptable for asymptomatic elderly; rhythm decisions are better owned by cardiology in this group.
  - Added: 2026-05-27

## Infectious Disease
### Sepsis
- [ID: d41bc0] Surviving Sepsis Campaign 2021
  - Key Recommendation: 30mL/kg fluid bolus within 3 hours for sepsis-induced hypotension or lactate >= 4.
  - Decision: Deferred
  - Physician Notes: I use 20mL/kg for HF/ESRD patients to avoid volume overload.
  - Rationale: Standard 30mL/kg bolus risks volume overload in my HF/ESRD population; I defer the fixed volume in favor of weight-adjusted, response-guided resuscitation.
  - Added: 2026-05-27

### Diabetes
- [ID: 993158] Standards of Care in Diabetes- 2026
  - Physician Notes: In adults with type 2 diabetes who have heart failure (HF) (with either reduced or preserved ejection fraction), an SGLT2 inhibitor is recommended for both glycemic management and prevention of HF hospitalizations (irrespective of A1C)
