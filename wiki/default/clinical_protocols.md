# Clinical Protocols

## Diseases/Issues
### Heart failure exacerbation
- First-line: IV loop diuretic (Lasix). Goal urine output 1-2L/day
- Monitor: daily BMP (creatinine, K+), daily weights, strict I&Os
- Target: return to dry weight before discharge
- CXR on admission and before discharge
- Cardiology consult if: new reduced EF, considering ICD upgrade, hemodynamic instability
- Note: this patient population often has CKD — watch creatinine closely during diuresis

### Sepsis / bacteremia
- Blood cultures x2 before antibiotics
- Infectious disease consult for: Staph aureus bacteremia, fungemia, unclear source
- Antibiotic choice: narrow based on cultures as soon as sensitivities available

## Labs/Blood Work
### Sodium abnormalities
- Hyponatremia workup: serum osm, urine osm, urine sodium first
- Correction rate: no faster than 6-8 mEq/L per 24h (risk of osmotic demyelination)
- Always flag for PCP follow-up at 1 week post-discharge

## Imaging
### Incidental imaging findings
- Any incidental finding requiring follow-up must appear in transitional issues
- Specify the follow-up modality, timeframe, and responsible provider
- Do not leave incidental findings unaddressed

## Medications
### Anticoagulation
- Always verify indication, duration, and current INR (if warfarin) before discharge
- Bridge therapy decisions: discuss with attending
